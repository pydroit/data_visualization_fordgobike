Part I - Ford GoBike System Analysis¶

by Oluwatobi Ajao¶

Introduction¶

This dataset includes a 2019 information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area. Details such as the total duration of each subscriber's (or customer's) trip is located alongside the location where the trip started and where the trip ended.

The dataset is a single csv file which will be assessed, cleaned and analyzed

Preliminary Wrangling¶

In this section, the data will be visually and programmatically assessed to have an understanding of the data. Issues arising from this assessment will then be cleaned/wrangled before analysis and visualization is carried out.

After this, the structure and features of the dataset will be evaluated and discussed.

Structure of the dataset¶

The dataset has 183215 bike trips and 19 fields describing each field.
The trips that span over 36 thousand hours were done in 329 stations.
The columns are mostly float and string, except the duration_sec which is understandably integer

My main features of interest in the dataset¶

I will like to know the demographics of the bike riders; their age group and gender.
I would also ike to the time of the day they mostly make rides and also the top patronised stations.

Features in the dataset that will help support my investigation into my features of interest¶

For those who have identified with a gender, and specified their birth year, the member_birth_year and member_gender columns will help give insights to the demogaphics of the customers/subscribers.
Fields like the start_time, end_time, start_station_id will help identify the behaviours of the riders as regards to the top time of the day and stations rides were made.

Trips by Weekend vs Weekday hours¶

We've seen most trips take place in the mornings and evenings. Delving deeper, how does this play out in the weekdays against weekends where there is expected to be vairiations in the daily commitment hours.

The analysis shows that as expected, riders make rides during weekends between morning and afternoon, with peak hours at mid-day. This is contrary to weekdays that have two peak periods; morning (between 7am and 9am) and evening (between 4pm and 7pm).
